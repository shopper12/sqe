## FLK1 sample set

### 1. Business medium

A client wants to start a trading business alone. She wants minimal filing duties and full control, but she is worried that suppliers may sue if the business fails. What is the key disadvantage of remaining a sole trader?

A. The business cannot make contracts.
B. The client has unlimited personal liability.
C. The business must have a board.
D. The client must file company accounts.
E. The business cannot employ staff.

Answer: B.

Explanation: A sole trader contracts personally and bears personal liability. The other options describe companies or are incorrect.

### 2. Apparent authority

A company tells a supplier that its operations manager handles all equipment purchases. The manager signs a purchase contract within that description, although the board had privately limited the manager's authority. What is the strongest argument that the company is bound?

A. The manager always has unlimited authority.
B. The supplier may rely on the company's representation of authority.
C. A private board limit is a criminal penalty.
D. The contract is void automatically.
E. The supplier becomes a shareholder.

Answer: B.

Explanation: Apparent authority depends on a representation from the principal and reliance by the third party.

### 3. Contract terms

A buyer signs a written contract containing standard terms. She did not read the exclusion clause. There is no misrepresentation about the document. What is the starting point?

A. The clause is ignored because unread.
B. The signed document normally binds the signer.
C. Only handwritten terms bind.
D. The contract fails for lack of consideration.
E. The seller must prove the buyer understood each word.

Answer: B.

Explanation: Signature usually incorporates terms, subject to controls and special facts.

### 4. Civil procedure stage

A client has a strong claim but limitation will expire very soon. Settlement talks are continuing. What should the solicitor consider most urgently?

A. Wait until negotiations end.
B. Issue proceedings or secure a valid standstill arrangement.
C. Prepare only the trial bundle.
D. Apply for sentencing.
E. Register title at HM Land Registry.

Answer: B.

Explanation: Negotiation alone does not safely stop limitation.

### 5. Negligence structure

A claimant proves careless conduct but cannot prove that the loss was caused by that conduct. Which element is most directly missing?

A. Duty.
B. Breach.
C. Causation.
D. Capacity.
E. Registration.

Answer: C.

Explanation: Breach without causal connection is insufficient.

## FLK2 sample set

### 6. Registered land

A buyer is purchasing registered land. Another person is visibly living at the property and claims an equitable interest. What should the buyer's solicitor treat as the immediate risk area?

A. Possible overriding interest.
B. Company share allotment.
C. Part 36 costs.
D. Testamentary capacity only.
E. Office money transfer.

Answer: A.

Explanation: Actual occupation may protect certain interests in registered land.

### 7. Conveyancing sequence

After exchange of contracts in a standard sale, what is the core practical effect?

A. The parties normally become contractually bound to complete.
B. The buyer automatically becomes registered proprietor.
C. The will is revoked.
D. The solicitor may ignore lender instructions.
E. The seller becomes a trustee of all buyer funds.

Answer: A.

Explanation: Exchange creates binding contractual obligations; registration comes later.

### 8. Wills witness

A will is validly executed, but one witness is also a beneficiary under the will. What is the usual risk?

A. The whole will must always fail.
B. The gift to the witness-beneficiary may fail.
C. The executor becomes a director.
D. The estate becomes registered land.
E. The witness becomes a trustee of every asset.

Answer: B.

Explanation: The will may remain valid while the witness-beneficiary's gift is affected.

### 9. Trust certainty

A settlor uses words that do not show an intention to create binding obligations. Which certainty is most directly missing?

A. Certainty of intention.
B. Certainty of court venue.
C. Certainty of limitation period.
D. Certainty of company name.
E. Certainty of criminal sentence.

Answer: A.

Explanation: A trust requires certainty of intention, subject matter, and objects.

### 10. Client money

A firm receives money from a client on account of future fees and disbursements before any bill is delivered. What is the starting classification?

A. Client money.
B. Office money automatically.
C. Share capital.
D. Damages.
E. Probate grant.

Answer: A.

Explanation: Money held for future work is normally client money until properly billed or transferred under the rules.
