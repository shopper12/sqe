## Study method

SQE1 tests application of rules to client facts. For each topic, learn the rule, the trigger facts, the exception, the likely trap, and the professional conduct point.

## FLK1 overview

### Business Law and Practice

A sole trader has no separate legal personality. A company limited by shares has separate legal personality, limited shareholder liability, directors, shareholders, constitutional documents, and filing duties. Partnerships create mutual agency risks. The exam often asks which business medium fits liability, control, tax, investment, and succession facts.

Directors must act within powers, promote company success, use independent judgment, apply reasonable care, avoid conflicts, refuse unauthorised benefits, and declare interests. The trap is that profit does not cure a defective process.

### Dispute Resolution

Civil procedure questions usually turn on stage: pre-action, issue, service, response, allocation, disclosure, evidence, trial, settlement, costs, and enforcement. Limitation must be checked early. The trap is choosing a merits answer when the best answer is the next procedural step.

### Contract

Formation requires offer, acceptance, consideration, intention, and certainty. Terms may be express or implied. Breach remedies usually aim to place the claimant in the performance position, subject to causation, remoteness, mitigation, and certainty.

### Tort

Negligence requires duty, breach, causation, remoteness, and loss. Occupiers and nuisance questions depend on control, foreseeability, reasonableness, and the claimant's status or land interest.

### Legal System and Legal Services

Public law asks whether the decision-maker had power, used fair process, and acted lawfully. Legal services questions add duties of honesty, integrity, independence, confidentiality, client service, and duties to the court.

## FLK2 overview

### Land Law and Property Practice

Land questions require identifying the estate or interest, then checking creation, formality, registration, priority, and remedies. Conveyancing questions follow instruction, title review, searches, enquiries, exchange, completion, post-completion and registration.

### Wills and Estates

A valid will requires capacity, intention, formal execution, and no invalidating factor. Personal representatives collect assets, pay debts and tax, and distribute the estate.

### Trusts

Trust creation requires certainty of intention, subject matter, and objects. Trustees must comply with the trust terms, avoid conflicts, invest properly, act with care, and account for trust property.

### Criminal Liability and Practice

Liability questions require matching facts to offence elements and available defences. Practice questions follow investigation, station advice, charge, bail, first appearance, allocation, trial, sentence, and appeal.

### Solicitors Accounts

The first step is classifying money as client money or office money. Then apply the rules on receipts, transfers, withdrawals, bills, ledgers, and residual balances.

## Last-day revision sheet

For each weak topic, write six lines: rule, trigger, exception, trap, ethics point, and one example.
