Method

For each topic: rule, trigger, trap, ethics, application.

FLK1

Business Law and Practice

Sole traders carry personal liability. Companies have separate legal personality and limited shareholder liability. Partnerships create agency risk. Directors duties are tested through authority, conflicts, company benefit, and process defects.

Dispute Resolution

Map every civil question to the stage of the claim: pre-action, issue, service, response, allocation, evidence, trial, costs, and enforcement. Limitation and settlement consequences are high-frequency traps.

Contract

Formation, terms, breach, and remedies must be applied to the facts. The best answer often turns on offer/acceptance, incorporation, classification of terms, remoteness, mitigation, or measure of loss.

Tort

Negligence requires duty, breach, causation, remoteness, and loss. Occupiers and nuisance questions turn on control, reasonableness, foreseeability, and claimant status.

Legal System and Legal Services

Public law focuses on power, process, and legality. Legal services questions add confidentiality, conflicts, undertakings, client money, independence, and duties to the court.

FLK2

Land and Property Practice

Identify the estate or interest, then creation, formality, registration, priority, and remedy. Conveyancing follows instruction, searches, enquiries, exchange, completion, post-completion and registration.

Wills and Estates

Will validity depends on capacity, intention, execution, and absence of invalidating factors. Estate administration requires collecting assets, paying liabilities and tax, and distributing correctly.

Trusts

Trusts require certainty and proper constitution. Trustees must avoid conflicts, act within powers, invest properly, keep accounts, and respect beneficiary interests.

Criminal Liability and Practice

Liability requires matching facts to elements and defences. Practice follows investigation, advice, charge, bail, first appearance, allocation, trial, sentence, and appeal.

Solicitors Accounts

Classify money first. Then apply the rules on receipts, transfers, withdrawals, bills, ledgers, and residual balances.
